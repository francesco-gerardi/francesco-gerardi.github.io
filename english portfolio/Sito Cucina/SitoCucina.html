The savory borage pie is a filled puff pastry that is as simple as it is delicious. A cake that is the classic wild dish, good as an appetizer, as a vegetarian second course, cut into squares at aperitif time and to take away for a packed lunch. Borage is a spontaneous edible herb, and also easily cultivable, which grows between spring and summer. Characterized by large, rather substantial leaves covered in down, it is widely used in Ligurian cuisine, especially as ingredients for fillings. In this recipe it is freshly blanched and mixed with ricotta, eggs and cheese, as well as in the tasty Genovese pansotti.

Italian regional cuisine

The regional recipes, whether from Campania, Puglia, Marche or Piedmont, offer a consistent culinary variety in the panorama of traditional Italian cuisine. The dishes, which have made and still make the history of Italian regional cuisine, are traditional dishes, handed down for generations. Fried, pan-fried or baked pizzas; mature, fresh or semi-mature cheeses, simple or stuffed focaccias, piadinas, desserts, etc. these are just some of the proposals that Italian regional cuisines offer us. In short, going to discover regional dishes is equivalent to taking a real culinary journey through the colours, scents and memories of an Italian culinary tradition that still leads the world today.
